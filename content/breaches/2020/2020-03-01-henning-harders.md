Henning Harders, an Australian logistics and warehousing company, became another victim in a wave of ransomware attacks targeting the transport and logistics sector.

### What Happened

Henning Harders fell victim to a ransomware attack that encrypted company systems and disrupted operations. The attack followed a pattern of targeting logistics companies, with Toll Group having been hit just weeks earlier. Attackers deployed ransomware that locked critical business systems and demanded payment for decryption keys.

The incident affected the company's ability to process shipments and manage warehouse operations, though the full extent of the disruption was not publicly disclosed.

### Impact on Individuals

The primary impact was operational disruption affecting business customers relying on Henning Harders' logistics services. The extent of personal data exposure, if any, was not publicly disclosed.

### Organisational Response

Henning Harders worked to restore systems and resume normal operations. The incident highlighted the logistics sector's vulnerability to ransomware attacks and the need for robust cybersecurity measures across supply chain operations.
