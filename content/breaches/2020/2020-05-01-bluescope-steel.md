BlueScope Steel, Australia's largest steel manufacturer, suffered a suspected ransomware attack that disrupted IT systems and impacted production operations.

### What Happened

BlueScope Steel experienced what was widely believed to be a ransomware attack affecting its IT infrastructure. The company described it as an "IT disruption" but security experts and media reports indicated the incident bore the hallmarks of ransomware.

The attack impacted production systems at BlueScope's operations, potentially affecting steel manufacturing processes and supply chain coordination. The company worked to contain the incident and restore systems while maintaining critical operations.

### Impact on Individuals

While primarily affecting business operations, the disruption had potential flow-on effects to BlueScope employees and customers in the construction and manufacturing sectors relying on steel supplies.

### Organisational Response

BlueScope engaged cybersecurity specialists to investigate and remediate the incident. The company worked to restore systems from backups and implemented enhanced security measures. The incident highlighted the vulnerability of critical manufacturing infrastructure to cyber attacks.
