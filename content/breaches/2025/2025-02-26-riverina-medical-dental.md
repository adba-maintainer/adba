## Summary

Riverina Medical and Dental Aboriginal Corporation, a Wagga Wagga-based healthcare provider serving Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander communities, confirmed a cyber incident in late February 2025. The organisation stated the incident may have involved personal data but has been contained.

## What Happened

In late February 2025, Riverina Medical and Dental Aboriginal Corporation detected a cyber attack affecting their systems. The incident occurred during a particularly challenging period for Australia's healthcare sector, coinciding with a wave of attacks that included ransomware incidents at fertility clinic Genea and Pound Road Medical Centre in Victoria. The organisation immediately commenced an investigation into the incident.

## Impact on Individuals

The organisation has indicated that personal data may have been involved in the incident, though specific details about the nature and extent of compromised information have not been publicly disclosed. The healthcare provider serves Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander people in Wagga Wagga and surrounding communities, as well as providing outreach services to Brungle.

## Organisational Response

Riverina Medical and Dental Aboriginal Corporation confirmed the incident has been contained and stated that an investigation is underway to determine the full scope of the breach. The organisation provides a range of healthcare services to vulnerable communities and continues to operate while addressing the cyber incident.
